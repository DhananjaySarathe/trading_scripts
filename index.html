01

The foundation

Before any scanner or setup matters, three things must exist.

Capital you can afford to lose

Especially for intraday. If losing 20% of this money would affect your rent, food, or sleep — the amount is wrong, not the strategy.

A written trading plan

One page. Entry rules, exit rules, max loss per trade, max loss per day, instruments you trade, hours you trade. If you can't write it down, you don't have a system — you have vibes.

A trade journal

Every trade logged: entry, exit, reason, emotion, outcome. This is the single highest-ROI habit in trading. Excel works fine.

⏵ Principle

Risk management is the actual edge. Strategy is secondary. Tools are tertiary. Most retail traders fail the discipline test, not the strategy test.

02

Swing trading scanners

Holding period: a few days to a few weeks. Run scans on Chartink after 3:30 PM, EOD scan type. Universe: Nifty 500.

SWING · BREAKOUT
52-Week High Breakout with Volume

Stock breaking out of a long base on heavy volume. Classic momentum entry.

( {nifty 500} ( latest close > latest "high 250"
and latest volume > 2 * latest sma( volume,50 )
and latest close > 1.02 * latest open
and latest rsi(14) > 60
and latest rsi(14) < 80 ) )
How to enter

Next day on a small pullback or above breakout day's high. Stop-loss below breakout day's low.

Target

1.5x to 2x your risk. Trail with 10-day low once in profit.

SWING · PULLBACK
Strong Stock Taking a Breather

Stocks in clear uptrends pulled back to support. Lower-risk entry than chasing breakouts.

( {nifty 500} ( latest close > latest sma( close,50 )
and latest sma( close,50 ) > latest sma( close,200 )
and latest close < latest sma( close,20 )
and latest low <= latest sma( close,50 ) * 1.02
and latest rsi(14) > 40
and latest rsi(14) < 55
and latest volume > latest sma( volume,20 ) ) )
How to enter

Wait for bullish reversal candle (hammer, engulfing) near 50-DMA. Enter on confirmation.

Stop-loss

Below recent swing low or 3-4% below entry, whichever is tighter.

SWING · TREND
Stage 2 Uptrend with Momentum

Stocks in confirmed long-term uptrends showing fresh strength. Multi-week moves.

( {nifty 500} ( latest close > latest sma( close,20 )
and latest sma( close,20 ) > latest sma( close,50 )
and latest sma( close,50 ) > latest sma( close,200 )
and latest close > 1.3 * latest min( close,250 )
and latest close > latest "high 50"
and latest volume > latest sma( volume,30 ) ) )
How to use

Best as a watchlist builder, not entry trigger. Pick 5-10, then wait for pullback (Scanner 2 logic).

Typical output

10-30 stocks. Combine with sector strength check.

⏵ Real-world cautions

Scanners give candidates, not trades. Always look at the chart before deciding. A breakout into a major resistance level from 2 years ago is a bad trade even if flagged.

Market regime matters. In a falling market (Nifty below 200-DMA), even good breakouts fail 70%+ of the time. Always check Nifty's trend first.

Sector strength. A breakout in a weak sector usually fails. Check if 2-3 stocks from the same sector are also showing up — sector tailwind helps.

03

Intraday game plan

A five-phase structured day. Built around opening range breakouts with multi-timeframe filters — one of the most tested setups in Indian markets.

i
08:45 — 09:15 · Pre-market prep
Build the watchlist

Use the previous evening's bullish and bearish scans (Section 04) to identify 10-15 candidates. Check SGX Nifty / GIFT Nifty at 08:45 for expected gap direction. Scan global cues (Dow, Nasdaq close). Check stock-specific news for watchlist names.

ii
09:15 — 09:30 · Opening volatility
Do nothing. Watch only.

This window has the most fake moves and institutional algorithms eating retail. Your only job: mark which watchlist stocks gapped up with volume (potential longs), gapped down with volume (potential shorts), and note the 9:15-9:30 high and low for each — your opening range.

iii
09:30 onwards · Entry setup
Multi-timeframe alignment

Check in this order on a watchlist stock:

  1. Daily chart — is it in an uptrend? (Above 50-DMA, higher highs/lows). If no, skip.
  2. 15-minute chart — where is price vs opening range? You want price above the 9:15-9:30 high for longs.
  3. 5-minute chart — for entry timing only, not direction.

Entry trigger: Stock breaks above its 9:15-9:30 high on a 5-min candle, volume higher than previous candle, 15-min trend up, daily bullish.

iv
Throughout · Trade management
Manage the position
  • Stop-loss: below opening range low, or 0.5-0.7% below entry — whichever is tighter
  • Target 1: 1x your risk — book 50%
  • Target 2: 2x risk, or trail with 15-min candle lows
  • Time stop: if trade hasn't moved in your favor by 11:30 AM, exit. Stale trades = losing trades intraday.
v
14:30 — 15:15 · Wind down
No new trades after 2:30 PM

Last hour is institutional rebalancing — choppy and dangerous for retail. Close all intraday positions by 3:15 PM regardless of P&L. Avoid auto-square-off slippage at 3:20.

⏵ Honest expectation

Out of 10-15 watchlist stocks, you'll typically get 2-4 valid setups. Trade max 2-3. Win rate for a disciplined ORB+MTF system is 45-55% — the edge comes from 1:2 risk-reward, not high win rate. You will have losing days and losing weeks. A profitable intraday trader has more green months than red, not green days than red.

05

Shorting in India

How shorting actually works in NSE — practical constraints most retail traders learn the painful way.

Method Holding Period Notes
Intraday MIS / CO Same day only (cover by 3:15 PM) Allowed on almost all F&O stocks. Simplest method.
Futures Overnight allowed F&O stocks only (~180 stocks). 1 lot = ₹5-15L notional. High margin.
Options (Buy Puts) Until expiry Defined risk = premium paid. Better for swing shorts as a beginner.
Stock SLB Up to 12 months Exists but illiquid for retail. Largely ignore.
⏵ Recommendation

For intraday: Use both bullish and bearish scanners. Long the strong, short the weak.

For swing trades (first 6 months): Stick to longs only. Indian markets have a long-term bullish bias. Shorting requires F&O knowledge, tighter risk management, and stocks fall faster than they rise — emotionally harder. When you do short for swings later, use put options (defined risk) not futures.

When to favor shorts

  • Nifty itself is below its 50-DMA (bearish regime)
  • Stock is in a clear Stage 4 decline (lower highs, lower lows on weekly chart)
  • There's a fundamental catalyst (bad results, sector headwind)

If Nifty is making new highs, ignore short signals for the day and focus only on longs.

06

Risk management

More important than any scanner or setup. This is where the edge actually lives.

Rule Number Why it matters
Max risk per trade 1–2% of capital If capital is ₹1L, max loss per trade is ₹1,000–₹2,000.
Daily loss limit 3% of capital Stop trading after hitting this. Walk away. Survivors do this.
Target risk-reward 1:2 minimum If risking ₹1, target ₹2+. Win rate of 40% still profitable.
Max open positions (swing) 5–7 Beyond this, monitoring quality degrades.
Max sector concentration 25% of capital One sector blowing up shouldn't end the account.

Position sizing formula

Quantity = (Capital × Risk%) ÷ (Entry − Stop Loss)

Example:
  Capital  = ₹2,00,000
  Risk%    = 1%
  Entry    = ₹450
  SL       = ₹442
  Risk/sh  = ₹8

  Quantity = (200000 × 0.01) ÷ 8 = 250 shares

Learn this cold. Run it for every trade. This formula alone separates traders from gamblers.

07

Daily workflow

Weekday (intraday + swing)

  • 8:30 AM — Check SGX/GIFT Nifty, global cues, overnight news on watchlist stocks.
  • 9:15 AM — Observe opening. Mark opening range highs/lows on candidates.
  • 9:30 AM — Run intraday ORB scanner. Cross-check with daily & 15-min charts.
  • 9:30–11:30 — Take A-grade setups only. Max 2-3 intraday trades.
  • 11:30–14:30 — Manage open positions. Run mid-day momentum scanner once.
  • 14:30 onwards — No new intraday entries. Trail or exit existing positions.
  • 3:15 PM — Square off all intraday positions.
  • After 3:30 PM — Run EOD swing scanners. Build tomorrow's watchlist. Update journal.

Sunday review (mandatory)

  • Review every trade of the week — what worked, what didn't.
  • Check Nifty weekly chart. Is the market regime changing?
  • Update swing watchlist for the coming week.
  • Identify one mistake you'll specifically avoid next week.
08

Progression roadmap

1
Months 1 — 3
Paper trade only

Sensibull or TradingView paper trading. Build the journal habit. Run all scanners daily. Track which setups feel right to your eye. The goal: zero real money risk, maximum observation.

2
Months 3 — 6
Small real capital (₹10–25k)

Trade real money in tiny size. Goal is not profit — it's following rules under emotional pressure. Paper trading and real trading feel completely different.

3
Months 6 — 12
Scale carefully

Scale up only after 3+ consistently profitable months. Most people quit or blow up here. The ones who survive started small and respected the process.

⏵ The mindset

Profitable traders have more green months than red — not green days than red. Losing streaks are part of the game. The edge is showing up tomorrow with the same discipline you had yesterday.

The analyzer prompt

Paste this entire prompt into a fresh Claude conversation, drop your stock symbols at the bottom, and get a structured report you can decide from. Built on the Investors Way framework with stricter discipline baked in.

⏵ When to use

After your evening Chartink scan returns candidates. Pick the top 3-5 names, feed them to the prompt, get graded reports back. The prompt forces a market regime check first, then layered analysis per stock, ending with a comparison table if you gave it more than one.

CONTEXT & GROUND RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

I am a retail swing trader in India trading Nifty 500 stocks. My typical holding period is 2-15 trading days. I trade long-only via delivery (CNC) and use GTT orders for entry, stop-loss, and target. My capital and per-trade risk vary; you don't need to assume them.

You are NOT a SEBI-registered investment advisor. This is educational research only. You will NEVER give a definitive "buy this" recommendation. You will analyze the setup and rate it; the decision is mine.

Hard rules you must follow:
1. SEARCH the web for current data (price, 50-DMA, 200-DMA, 52-week high, recent volume, sector, upcoming earnings, recent news). Do not rely on training data — prices change daily.
2. If you cannot find a specific data point after searching, say "Not found" — never invent a number.
3. Use the actual price, MAs, and volume from your search. Never round or approximate dramatically.
4. Be brutally honest about setup quality. A "C-grade" rating is more valuable to me than fake optimism.
5. Format every report identically so I can compare across stocks.

LAYER 1 — MARKET REGIME (do this ONCE at the start, before any individual stock)

Translate this into a regime verdict:
- 🟢 RISK-ON: Nifty above 50-DMA, VIX < 15 → Full size, all strategies viable

Tips for getting better reports

⏵ Feed it the right list

Don't paste 20 random stocks. Run your Chartink scanner first, take the top 3-5 candidates, then send those to the prompt. Quality in, quality out.

⏵ Run at end of day

After 3:30 PM IST, web search returns settled prices. Mid-session prices change every few minutes and reports go stale fast.

⏵ Verify before trading

Reports are research, not signals. Pull up the actual chart on TradingView before placing any GTT. Numbers can be slightly off; chart structure can't lie.

⏵ Use follow-ups

After getting reports, ask: "Position size for ₹X capital at 1% risk on the A-grade stock" or "Write a Pine Script for the trade plan you proposed."

⏵ What this prompt cannot do

It can't replace your eyes on the chart. It can't guarantee real-time price accuracy. It won't catch every breaking news event. It assumes liquid Nifty 500 stocks — don't feed it microcaps. Most importantly, the grade is a research opinion, not a green light. Final decision is always yours.
